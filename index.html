Narratives / Reflection

A Symphony of Learning at Eusebio High School

Field Study 2 turned Eusebio High School onto the stage for my academic journey. I had no idea that this experience would create a tapestry of emotions, obstacles, and significant progress when I entered the hallways full of the eager hum of adolescent energy.

My task was straightforward: I was given the responsibility of teaching Grade 7 children from sections Australia and Brunei about Computer System Servicing, and I was given the task of teaching Grade 7 students from Canada, Denmark, and Oman about Electrical. Not only were these portions classrooms, but they also served as testing grounds, creative hubs, and eventually, transformational spaces.

Australia and Brunei: The Pioneers of Computer System Servicing

There was a wide range of feelings on the first day with Brunei and Australia. In our capacity as the Computer System Servicing pilot sections, the students and I set off on an unknown adventure. I still clearly recall the glimmer in their eyes as we explored the complexities of computer hardware, and software, and the wonders that occur inside its circuitry.

Together, we made our way through the maze of coding, debugging, and sometimes small victories that are understood only by techies. With interactive lessons and hands-on activities, the classroom became a haven of mutual discoveries. The students not only became scholars but also trailblazers, laying the groundwork for subsequent generations to come.

Canada, Denmark, and Oman: The Electric Symphony

Weeks passed, and my focus finally went to sections Canada, Denmark, and Oman, where circuits and electrons were the languages being spoken. It was thrilling to teach electrical to these inquisitive minds. Every course was a step into the fascinating realm of knowledge, covering everything from the fundamentals of circuit design to the mysteries of electromagnetic.

As we examined the intricacies of electrical systems, there was a murmur of excitement around the classroom. We soldered circuits, made crafts, and were amazed at the transformations electricity could bring about. Notwithstanding their disparate backgrounds and points of view, the students were united by their common comprehension of electrons flowing through conductors.

Reflection: Tears of Triumph and Growth

Memories of my experience at Eusebio High School elicit a range of feelings. When a subject appeared unattainable, there were times when I felt frustrated, yet my pupils' tenacity and dedication matched mine. Tears were not only shed because of the difficulties, but also because of the triumph over obstacles, false beliefs, and the group development that took place in that classroom.

My experience instructing pilot sections in computer system servicing made me realize the need to have a pioneering mindset. By working together, we established a trail of curiosity and wisdom for others to follow. Those were not merely teacher-student bonds; they were also the bonds of fellow explorers plunging into the unknown world of technology.

The variety of sections in Electrical from Canada, Denmark, and Oman added a wealth of viewpoints to the classroom. We have found the universal language of science and creativity, which cuts beyond national boundaries and cultural differences, through the prism of electrons.

The emotional moments were sparked not just by difficulties but also by group victories over ignorance, the happiness that comes from watching a lightbulb flicker to life, and the realization that knowledge has no geographical bounds. Not only am I crying to say goodbye to Eusebio High School, but I'm also crying with appreciation for the opportunity to be a part of their academic path.

The memories of Eusebio High School will always be etched in my heart as the place where tears of confusion turned into tears of understanding, where the sound of electrical circuits symphonized with the click of keyboards, and where each lesson demonstrated the profoundly transforming power of education, leaving a lasting impression on my spirit.

Activities

With a dynamic teaching style, I design engaging exercises that push the boundaries of traditional learning for my students. For example, in the field of Computer System Servicing (CSS), I create a curious and exploratory environment by having students play a guessing game where they must figure out components that are essential to cascading style sheets (CSS). This allows students to gain practical experience while also learning theory.

Meanwhile, in the field of electronics, I skillfully incorporate the excitement of speculation into the educational process by having students play a guessing game for their electrical projects. This encourages students to identify and forecast components that are essential to finishing their projects successfully, which builds anticipation and critical thinking skills.

Moreover, in the area of practical application, Electrical students will have a stimulating experience when they work on the project of making extension cords, which aims to combine theoretical concepts with concrete results. This enhances their technical proficiency and fosters a profound understanding of the complex procedures involved in the sector. The CSS curriculum is integrated with the first-quarter Typing project, which requires students to create an extension cord. This adds another level of complexity and relevance to the learning process and demonstrates how different disciplines and skill sets are interconnected in the educational setting.
